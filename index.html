Strategic Violence: A Re-evaluation

The narrative of "state terror" is challenged by evidence of calculated, protester-initiated violence designed to provoke a state response and justify the regime change.

  • Confession of Strategy: "If the metro rail was not set on fire, or the police were not killed, the revolution would not have been achieved so easily." - Coordinator Hasibul Islam (Source 5)
  • Targeting Police: Evidence documents the brutal killing of over 44 police officers, with 700-800 reported missing. (Source 1)
  • Economic Sabotage: Systemic destruction of state infrastructure, including the Ministry of Transport (Setu Bhaban) and Metro Rail stations. (Source 1, 5)

The 7.62mm "Sniper" Mystery

A critical piece of evidence suggesting a "false flag" operation. 7.62mm military-grade bullets, not standard for police, were found (Source 1). The interim government's own Home Advisor, Brig. Gen. (Rtd.) Sakhawat Hossain, questioned this: "Does that mean we had armed civilians?" He was subsequently removed from his post, suggesting a high-level cover-up of a third-party sniper role.

Conclusion: A Fabricated Narrative

What the Evidence Suggests

The "July '24 Uprising" was not a simple student protest. It was a pre-planned political operation (Source 2, 3) that used a valid grievance as a "Trojan Horse." It relied on strategic, calculated violence (Source 5), a covert alliance with organized extremist groups (Source 7, 8, 9), and a "false flag" sniper operation (Source 1) to create the narrative of a "massacre."

The Aftermath: Immunity & Erasure

The new government provided legal immunity for all crimes committed *during* the uprising (Source 1). Simultaneously, it ignored the wave of post-uprising violence, including the systematic destruction of 1,500 secular sculptures and targeted attacks on Hindu minorities (Source 1). The UN report (Source 1), by using a biased timeline and falsified data, provided international cover for this unconstitutional change.
